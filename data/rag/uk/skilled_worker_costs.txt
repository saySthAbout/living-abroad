When you apply for a Skilled Worker visa, you'll need to have enough money to: pay the application fee (standard fee ranges from £819 to £1,865 depending on circumstances), pay the healthcare surcharge (usually £1,035 per year), and support yourself when you arrive in the UK (usually at least £1,270 available, unless exempt). You'll pay a lower application fee if your job is on the immigration salary list.

Application fees if applying from outside the UK: up to 3 years — £819 per person; more than 3 years — £1,618 per person. If applying from inside the UK to extend, switch or update: up to 3 years — £943 per person; more than 3 years — £1,865 per person. If your partner or child applies to join you, they pay the fee for the same length of time as you.

If your job is on the immigration salary list, you and your family pay a lower application fee: £628 for up to 3 years, or £1,235 for more than 3 years, regardless of whether applying from inside or outside the UK.

Money to support yourself: you must have at least £1,270 in your bank account, available for at least 28 days in a row, with day 28 within 31 days of applying. You'll usually need to show proof unless you've been in the UK with a valid visa for at least 12 months, or your employer can cover your costs during your first month (up to £1,270) — in which case your certificate of sponsorship must confirm this in the 'sponsor certifies maintenance' section under 'Additional data'. Your partner and children also need to prove they can support themselves.
