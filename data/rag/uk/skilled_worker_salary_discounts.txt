Skilled Worker visa: When you can be paid less

You might still be able to apply for a Skilled Worker visa if your job is eligible but your salary is less than the standard salary requirement of £41,700 or your job's standard 'going rate'.

You can be paid between 70% and 90% of the standard going rate for your job if your salary is at least £33,400 per year and you meet one of the following criteria:
you're under 26, studying or a recent graduate, or in professional training
you have a science, technology, engineering or maths (STEM) PhD level qualification that's relevant to your job (if you have a relevant PhD level qualification in any other subject your salary must be at least £37,500)
you have a postdoctoral position in science or higher education

There are different salary rules if you work in some healthcare or education jobs, or you're extending, updating, or switching to this visa to become a prison officer.

Your job is on the immigration salary list

The immigration salary list is a list of skilled jobs which have lower salary requirements and a lower visa application fee. If your job is on the list, you must still be paid at least £33,400 per year and at least the standard going rate for your job. Whether a job is listed can depend on which part of the UK you'll be working in — UK-wide, or only in England, Scotland, Wales or Northern Ireland.

You're under 26, studying or a recent graduate, or in professional training

You can be paid 70% of your job's standard going rate if your salary will be at least £33,400 per year and one of the following applies:
you're under 26 on the date you apply
you're currently in the UK on a Student visa studying at bachelor's degree level or above (or you have been in the last 2 years)
you're currently in the UK on a Graduate visa (or you have been in the last 2 years)
you'll be working towards a recognised qualification in a UK regulated profession, or towards full registration/chartered status in the job you're being sponsored for

Your total stay in the UK cannot be more than 4 years if you apply for one of these reasons, including any time already spent on a Graduate visa.

You have a PhD level qualification that's relevant to your job

If your job is eligible for a PhD salary discount, you can be paid 80% of the going rate (STEM qualification, at least £33,400/year) or 90% of the going rate (non-STEM qualification, at least £37,500/year). You must have a UK PhD or an equivalent overseas doctorate-level qualification (verified through Ecctis if overseas), and be able to prove the qualification is relevant to the job.

You have a postdoctoral position in science or higher education

You can be paid 70% of your job's standard going rate if you'll be working in a postdoctoral position in one of a specific set of science/research/higher-education occupation codes (for example chemical scientists, biological scientists, biochemists and biomedical scientists, physical scientists, or higher education teaching professionals). Your total stay in the UK cannot be more than 4 years under this route, including any time already spent on a Graduate visa.
