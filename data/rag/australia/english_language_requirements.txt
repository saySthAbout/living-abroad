The Department of Home Affairs requires evidence of English language proficiency for some Australian visa applications. This helps effectively manage immigration risk and ensure visa holders can fully participate in the Australian community.

For tests taken on or after 7 August 2025, the department accepts test scores from: Cambridge C1 Advanced Test, Canadian English Language Proficiency Index Program General (CELPIP General), IELTS Academic (including One Skill Retake), IELTS General Training (including One Skill Retake), LANGUAGECERT Academic Test, Michigan English Test (MET, including Single Section Retake), Occupational English Test (OET), Pearson Test of English Academic (PTE Academic), and TOEFL iBT (must select 'Taking TOEFL for Australia' when registering).

For tests taken on or before 6 August 2025, the department still accepts: Cambridge English (CAE/C1 Advanced), IELTS, PTE Academic, OET, and TOEFL iBT. Results from a test taken on or before 6 August 2025 may be used as evidence until 6 August 2028, depending on the visa.

English proficiency levels used for visa purposes: Functional English, Vocational English, Competent English, Proficient English, Superior English. You must check what level of English you need and what evidence you must provide for the specific visa subclass.

Online test restrictions: the department does not accept evidence from English language tests that are delivered completely online (remote-proctored or at-home), including CELPIP Online, IELTS Online, LANGUAGECERT Academic Online, MET Digital (at-home), OET@Home, and TOEFL iBT Home Edition. Tests must take place in a secure testing centre.
